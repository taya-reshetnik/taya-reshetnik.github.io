Internet, as a disembodied form of communication, often assumed to be impersonal and untrustworthy. "Globalising intimacy" is an installation, visualising how the absence of physical proximity creates intimacy. The installation includes 2 screens, placed in 2 different distant spots. Each of them shows the same message. However, you can only read the full message, if both you and the person in front of the other screen stand still. If one of you is moving the message is distorted. If one of you is absent, there's a piece of text missing.

In collaboration with Dora Kerekes.
With special thanks to Edwin Jakobs, Lizzie Malcolm, Daniel Shiffman.

2016
Project "52.083967, 4.239759" is centered around the idea of impossibility of the absolute understanding between people, which is, in my opinion, - interpretation. Referring to the deconstraction theories, John Berger's ideas on mystification and other sources, the project "52.083967, 4.239759" is offering a viewer to follow the very intuitive 'paths' of interpretation, revealing one specific way of seeing.
Startiung with a simple representation like a drawing or photograph, 10 objects were taken out of their initial context. The end result turned into the installation, where all the interpresentations of the objects were being presented. "52.083967, 4.239759" is an attempt to understand and to explore a fragile intuitive process of communication, full of multilayered connections.

Publication and installation.
